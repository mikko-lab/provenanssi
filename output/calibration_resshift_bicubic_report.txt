  Mechanistic interpretation:
  ResShift samples the conditional posterior p(x|y) where y is the bicubic
  LR image. The null-space component of each rectified sample (the invented
  high-frequency texture beyond what y constrains) varies between seeds.
  Where y is ambiguous (complex texture, many plausible HR completions),
  the posterior spread is large AND the reconstruction error is large.
  Where y strongly constrains x (smooth regions, clean edges), the spread
  is small AND the error is small. This is calibrated uncertainty.

  ResShift + bicubic + natural images crosses the calibration threshold.
